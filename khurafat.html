Tells the story of a forbidden agreement and one form of deviation in Islamic belief between humans and the devil, something that is strongly forbidden in our holy religion.

The idea for this film was expressed by Syamsul Yusof in May 2009 while Bohsia: Jangan Pilih Jalan Hitam was being screened.

It is the fourth film directed by Syamsul and the 26th film produced by Skop Productions in collaboration with Grand Brilliance.

It is Syamsul's first attempt at the horror genre after focusing on the action genre for a long time.

A month before its premiere, the official trailer for the film Khurafat was uploaded to the Grand Brilliance YouTube account on 20 December 2010 and has recorded 94,283 views. In its first four days of release, Khurafat: Perjanjian Syaitan grossed RM2.5 million, topping the Malaysian box office chart for the weekend of 13–16 January 2011. The film remained at the top of the national box office chart in its second week (20–26 January 2011).

After almost three weeks of release, it was reported that Khurafat had grossed RM6.9 million as of 31 January, a huge achievement for the first horror film produced by Skop Productions. The film grossed RM8.08 million.
