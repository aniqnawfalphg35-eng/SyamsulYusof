KL Gangster is Syamsul Yusof's fifth film directed by him and starring himself.

With a cost of RM1.8 million including promotion, filming was carried out in attractive locations around Kuala Lumpur and took 35 days to complete.

On its first day of release, the film managed to collect RM 1.5 million. This figure reached RM6.2 million after four days, thus topping the Malaysian cinema box office charts in its first weekend (9 to 15 June 2011), surpassing Western films such as Super 8, X-Men: First Class and Kung Fu Panda 2.

Just after its second weekend, it was reported that the film KL Gangster had grossed a total of RM10.3 million, surpassing the record of RM8.18 million held by Metrowealth Movies Production's Ngangkung at the end of 2010.

Throughout its run, KL Gangster grossed a total of RM11.74 million, maintaining its title as the highest-grossing Malaysian film for two and a half years.
